John Hupalo - Co-Founder

John’s career in education spans more than 25 years including C-level experience with a publicly traded private student loan company, senior leadership positions in Education Finance Groups on Wall Street and working on Capitol Hill. He founded Invite Education February 2012.

He began his professional career in 1982 on the Washington staff of Congressman John E. Porter, a member of the Education and Labor subcommittee of the Appropriations Committee. From 1987-2003, he worked in or managed Education Finance Groups at Deutsche Bank, UBS, Salomon Smith Barney and Manufacturers Hanover Securities Corporation. He was the lead banker on more than $20 billion of student loan securitizations. In March 2003, he joined First Marblehead Corporation (FMD: NYSE) where he participated in the Initial Public Offering and held senior leadership positions including serving as Chief Financial Officer. In 2009, he co-founded a student loan restructuring business at Ramirez & Co.

He is a nationally recognized expert in education finance having testified before the United States Congress. He frequently speaks at conferences on education financing issues.

He has published articles in various publications on management and leadership in a crisis, and on education financing issues. Mr. Hupalo received his B.A. with Honors from Boston University in 1982 and an MBA in Finance from New York University’s Stern School of Business in 1987. He has been elected to the School Board in Bronxville New York, founded a not-for-profit in New York City, served as Chairman of the Finance Committee for the Carroll School in Lincoln, MA and the Board of Finance for the Wellesley Village Church where he is currently Deputy Treasurer. He is also a Member of the Boston University Athletic Director’s Council.

Uri Pomerantz - Advisor

Uri Pomerantz has been involved with founding, managing and funding financial services and technology ventures throughout his career. He is the founder and CEO of Bright Frontier Financial, Inc., which works with community financial institutions to provide an innovative student loan product for credit-worthy borrowers, lowering the cost of college financing for high quality graduates throughout the country.

Uri has been involved in financial services since he was a student at Stanford University, when he co-founded a microfinance fund (Jozoor Microfinance) that was the first non-partisan Israeli-Palestinian microfinance program, assembled an advisory board including Israel’s National Security Advisor and senior Palestinian officials and issuing loans in the West Bank and Gaza via partners including the YMCA, the Koret Foundation and Kiva.org.

After graduate school, he spent several years at McKinsey & Company in Southeast Asia, providing strategic consulting to a variety of clients, including formulating national education public policy for a Prime Minister’s office, leading a national broadband rollout program, and developing a mobile banking strategy for a major telco.

Uri also served as a Business Development Manager for Microsoft Corporation, managing long-term partnerships with Fortune 500 Companies based in New England, and after serving as a Program Manager in Microsoft’s Windows CE mobile device division.

He received a full-merit scholarship to complete a Masters of International Development from Harvard University, and jointly completed a MBA from Stanford Business School, during which time he worked at venture capital firms in South Africa and China, in investment banking at Goldman Sachs, and advised on the formation of Sierra Leone’s first private equity firm.

Peter Mazareas, PhD. - Co-Founder

Dr. Peter Mazareas is considered one of the nation’s foremost experts on college financing and a committed advocate for the national college savings movement.

He is the Chairman Emeritus of the College Savings Foundation – the leading Trade Association for the 529 college savings movement. He is the former Executive Director of the Massachusetts Educational Financing Authority, where he implemented some of the first in the nation programs for 529 college savings plans (UFund) and pre-paid college tuition programs (UPlan). He is regularly featured at national conferences and has been quoted and interviewed in a variety of national publications, radio and TV shows including The Wall Street Journal, USA Today, Kiplinger’s, CNN, PBS, Bob Brinker, Money Magazine, Smart Money Magazine, Investment News and Ignites, where he has also served as a guest columnist.

Dr. Mazareas has also served as Deputy Treasurer for the Commonwealth of Massachsuetts, and as a full-time Visiting Professor of Business at Boston College's Carroll Graduate School of Management where he taught courses on strategy and business development.

Dr. Mazareas received a B.A. from Harvard College, and both an M.B.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Chicago. He was selected as a Ford Foundation Fellow and awarded a Woodrow Wilson Internship. He currently serves as President of the Harvard Club of the North Shore.

Joe Wrinn - Advisor

Joe Wrinn is an independent consultant to higher education leaders, with 25 years of experience in university communications and related public affairs positioning. He has an extensive record of credibly representing academic institutions and promoting their values, while building consensus and positive relationships across all sectors of a university’s political hierarchy.

As director for 20 years of Harvard University’s Office of News and Public Affairs, Wrinn was responsible for protecting and advancing Harvard’s global reputation through the efforts of the office. In that role, he directed all facets of university communications including print, photo, video, audio, digital, web, and emerging social media platforms. He also served on task forces addressing the escalating costs of higher education and developing solutions to make administrative support more efficient in maintaining academic quality.

Wrinn brings an interest in all facets of the contemporary education landscape and is especially focused on the evolving funding and programming relationships between universities, local governments, and regional corporations in the US and abroad. He is a Senior Counsel to Morrissey & Co., a Pinnacle Worldwide reputation communications agency, and is affiliated with Academy Communications, a Boston area media relations firm that brings national attention to scholars.

An honors graduate of Syracuse University’s Newhouse School of Public Communications, he worked previously as a photojournalist for the New York Times and United Press International. He credits travel to 55 countries in furthering his education and gaining global perspective and context. He has been a guest lecturer at Harvard, Boston College, Northeastern University, Boston University, Emerson College, the Boston Bar Association, and the Harvard Management Company.
